What is Hydroelectric Energy?

Hydroelectric energy is electricity generated by harnessing the hydraulic potential of a river. It uses the force of falling water or flowing currents to move turbines.

Hydroelectric power plants are among the most efficient sources of renewable energy in the world.

How does it work?

The process usually involves a dam to store water in a reservoir. When released, the water flows through a pipe and spins a turbine, which is connected to a generator.

Advantages

  • Renewable and clean source.
  • Low operating and maintenance costs.
  • Ability to store energy in reservoirs.

Challenges

  • High initial construction costs.
  • Environmental impact on local ecosystems.
  • Dependence on rainfall levels.
